Physical Appearance
Such a device would typically look like a periodic, textured plate made of a rigid substrate, such as silicon for nanophotonics or precision-machined aluminum for acoustic waves. The 64x64 grid consists of 4,096 discrete unit cells, each containing a finely tuned sub-wavelength structure like a split-ring resonator, a Helmholtz cavity, or an optical waveguide. When activated, external transceivers read the far-field interference pattern generated by the resonating wells, allowing researchers to physically "measure" the mathematical factors based on wave amplitude peaks.
