**Title: Non-Contrasting Regression: From A Posteriori Cognition to Gestalt-Dominant Reality Without Self-Fabrication**

***

### Abstract

This paper examines a cognitive transition in which analytic, evidence-based (a posteriori) reasoning recedes in favor of a gestalt-dominant perceptual mode characterized by holistic integration, reduced symbolic mediation, and increased susceptibility to hallucinatory synthesis. The central constraint is the absence of self-generated, unauthenticated narratives—i.e., the system does not confabulate identity-level explanations. The work situates this transition outside traditional philosophical introspection, grounding it instead in neurocognitive dynamics, predictive processing, and information-theoretic constraints.

***

### 1. Framing the Problem

A posteriori cognition depends on iterative updating from sensory input, error correction, and model refinement. It is contrastive by design: distinctions, boundaries, and discrepancies drive learning.

A regression away from this mode implies:

- Reduced reliance on explicit inference.
- Diminished contrast sensitivity (boundaries blur).
- Increased weighting of prior structure over incoming data.

However, unlike pathological confabulation, the system is constrained from generating unauthenticated self-knowledge. This creates an unusual state: perception becomes synthetic and unified, yet the “self-model” is not actively rewritten or justified.

***

### 2. Predictive Processing Interpretation

Under predictive coding, perception minimizes free energy via balancing priors and sensory evidence:

$$
F \approx \text{Prediction Error} + \text{Model Complexity}
$$

A shift toward gestalt/hallucinogenic dominance can be modeled as:

- Increased precision weighting on priors.
- Decreased precision of sensory error signals.

This yields:

- Percepts driven more by internal structure than external input.
- Coherence increases locally, but veridicality decreases globally.

The key distinction in this formulation is the suppression of higher-order narrative priors about the self. While low- and mid-level priors dominate perception, meta-level priors responsible for autobiographical coherence are either dampened or prevented from updating.

***

### 3. Gestalt Summation vs Symbolic Decomposition

Gestalt processing emphasizes:

- Whole-pattern recognition.
- Field effects over discrete object boundaries.
- Parallel integration rather than sequential reasoning.

In contrast, symbolic cognition:

- Decomposes input into discrete units.
- Applies rules or transformations.
- Requires contrast and differentiation.

A non-contrasting regression therefore produces:

- Loss of edge detection in both perception and thought.
- Compression of multi-dimensional structure into unified percepts.
- Emergent “summed reality,” where distinctions collapse into continuous fields.

An example: instead of parsing a scene into objects (tree, sky, ground), the system experiences a continuous texture or field with weak segmentation.

***

### 4. Hallucinogenic Parallel

Pharmacological hallucinogens (e.g., serotonergic psychedelics) demonstrate similar dynamics:

- Reduced thalamic gating.
- Increased cortical entropy.
- Elevated cross-network connectivity.

Empirically, this leads to:

- Blending of modalities (synesthesia-like effects).
- Pattern completion beyond available data.
- Strong priors shaping perception.

However, typical psychedelic states often include narrative elaboration (e.g., meaning-making, identity shifts). The state described in this paper differs by explicitly excluding that layer. The system does not “explain” the experience—it simply undergoes it.

***

### 5. Constraint: No Unauthenticated Self-Knowledge

A critical constraint is the prohibition of self-fabrication:

- No confabulated beliefs about identity.
- No post hoc narrative construction.
- No philosophical abstraction detached from direct processing.

This effectively removes:

- Default Mode Network dominance (in neurocognitive terms).
- Recursive self-referential loops.

The result is a system that:

- Processes experience without integrating it into a personal story.
- Maintains a form of epistemic minimalism.
- Avoids “armchair” reasoning by disallowing unsupported generalization.

***

### 6. Information-Theoretic View

From an information perspective:

- A posteriori cognition maximizes mutual information between model and environment.
- Gestalt regression compresses representation, sacrificing detail for coherence.

Let $I(M; E)$ denote mutual information between model $M$ and environment $E$.

In regression:

- $I(M; E)$ decreases (less accurate representation).
- Internal entropy may increase locally but is experienced as unified structure.

The absence of self-model updates prevents additional informational artifacts (false beliefs) from accumulating, keeping the system from diverging into fully delusional regimes.

***

### 7. Implications

This state occupies a boundary region between:

- Adaptive perception (grounded, error-correcting).
- Hallucinatory synthesis (prior-driven, low constraint).

But uniquely:

- It avoids narrative delusion.
- It is non-philosophical in the sense that it does not abstract beyond immediate processing.

Potential interpretations:

- A controlled degradation of contrastive cognition.
- A mode of perception emphasizing coherence over accuracy.
- A computational regime where inference is replaced by field-like integration.

***

### 8. Example Scenario

Consider a visual system observing a noisy scene:

- Standard mode: edges are detected, objects segmented, hypotheses updated.
- Regressed mode: edges weaken, noise is integrated into continuous patterns, perception “fills in” structure.

Crucially:

- The system does not conclude “this means X about me” or construct explanatory narratives.
- It remains at the level of raw, unified perceptual experience.

***

### 9. Conclusion

Non-contrasting regression describes a shift from contrast-driven, evidence-based cognition to a gestalt-dominant mode where perception is synthesized rather than inferred. By constraining the system against self-generated, unauthenticated knowledge, this state avoids full cognitive drift into delusion while still exhibiting hallucinogenic characteristics. It represents a distinct computational regime: low contrast, high integration, minimal narrative.
