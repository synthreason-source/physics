# DIY CNT-Epoxy Mesh Guide

Build a ~1g, high-strength carbon nanotube (CNT) mesh reinforced with epoxy, suitable for lightweight lifting or e-textile prototypes. This uses accessible lab/DIY tools like ultrasonication and wet-spinning; expect 2–4 hours total and tensile strength ~1 GPa (lifting ~1 kg/g). [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5456468/)

## Materials (for 1g mesh)

- 0.2–0.5g MWCNTs or SWCNTs (powder, ~$50–200/g from Sigma-Aldrich or Cheap Tubes)  
- 1–2g low-viscosity epoxy resin (e.g., West System 105 + hardener, or DIY mix with bisphenol-A)  
- 5–10 mL surfactant solution (0.5 wt% SDS or Triton X-100 in water)  
- Coagulant bath: 5% HCl or acetone (500 mL)  
- Optional: 0.1g functionalized CNTs for better bonding  

## Equipment

- Ultrasonic bath/horn (e.g., 100W probe sonicator)  
- Syringe pump or peristaltic pump (0.1–1 mL/min)  
- Spinning needle/capillary (20–30G)  
- Hot plate/stirrer, oven (up to 165°C)  
- Tweezers, gloves, petri dishes, weave frame (3D-printed or cardboard loom)  

## Step 1: CNT Dispersion (30–45 min)

1. Disperse 0.2g CNTs in 5 mL 0.5 wt% SDS water via ultrasonication: 20 kHz, 100W, 30 min pulses (ice bath to avoid heat). Aim for stable black ink (viscosity ~1000 cP). [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8537922/)
2. Add 1g epoxy resin dropwise while stirring; sonicate another 15 min for pre-infiltration.  

## Step 2: Wet-Spin Yarn (15–30 min)

1. Load dispersion into 10 mL syringe on pump.  
2. Extrude at 0.3 mL/min through needle into HCl/acetone coagulant bath while rotating/stretching (manual twist-draw ratio 1.5–2). Wind ~2–5 m yarn (10–50 µm dia., ~0.2 g/m). [onlinelibrary.wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/adfm.202400197)
3. Rinse yarn in water, dry at 60°C (10 min).  

## Step 3: Weave Mesh (10–15 min)

1. Ply 5–10 yarns into thicker strands if needed.  
2. Weave/knit into ~10x10 cm mesh on frame (plain weave, ~1g total; use tweezers for tension). Secure intersections with dilute epoxy drops. [pubs.acs](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acsanm.4c06088)

## Step 4: Epoxy Infiltrate & Cure (1–2 hours)

1. Brush/dip mesh in 1:1 epoxy:hardener mix (ensure <1 wt% excess to avoid brittleness).  
2. Squeeze out excess, lay flat between glass plates.  
3. Cure: 25°C 1 hour → 80°C 1 hour → 165°C 30 min (ramp slowly). Cool slowly. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7660653/)

## Testing & Performance

- **Lift test**: Hang from hook; 1g mesh lifts 0.5–1 kg static (safety factor 2). Measure with digital scale.  
- **Tensile**: Clamp ends, pull with force gauge (~1 GPa expected). [pubs.acs](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acsanm.3c01266)
- Yields ~80% strength vs. lab-optimized; scale by parallel spinning.  

## Safety & Tips

- Fume hood for solvents/epoxy; gloves/N95 mask (CNTs are fine dust).  
- Functionalize CNTs (HNO3 boil 1h) for 20% strength boost if time allows. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6326190/)
- Troubleshoot: Clumpy dispersion? Longer sonication. Weak yarn? Higher draw ratio.  

Total cost ~$100–300 first run. For charge-oscillators, pattern electrodes pre-weave and suspend CNT bridges before epoxy. [sciencedirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0042207X22000112)
