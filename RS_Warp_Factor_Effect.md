The RS Warp Factor Effect
Why gravity appears incredibly weak in our domain and barely perturbs the test particles.
The simulation directly contrasts how these metric perturbations behave on the extra-dimensional Planck brane versus our visible TeV brane. On the Planck brane, the amplitude of the gravitational wave is at its maximum, leading to extreme distortions in the particle ring (indicated by the red outer lines). However, as the graviton propagates across the extra dimension to the TeV brane, its magnitude is reduced by the RS warp factor.
